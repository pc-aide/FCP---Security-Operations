Question 19

What are three key routing principles in SD-WAN? (Choose three.)






Explanation:

FGT 7.4 ASG documentation provides these principles:

  • A. SD-WAN members are only used if they have a valid route to the destination, ensuring effective path selection.
  • C. If the best route to a destination does not belong to an SD-WAN member, SD-WAN rules are skipped, which aligns with SD-WAN's primary purpose of using its own members.
  • E. Regular policy routes have precedence over SD-WAN rules.

References:

  • Fortinet SD-WAN Routing Logic - FortiOS 7.0.0
  • Fortinet Secure SD-WAN Architecture for MSSP
  • Fortinet SD-WAN Components and Design Principles - FortiOS 7.6.0
  • Sophos - SD-WAN Policy Routing

Correct Answers:
A. By default, SD-WAN members are skipped if they do not have a valid route to the destination
C. By default, SD-WAN rules are skipped if the best route to the destination is not an SD-WAN member
E. Regular policy routes have precedence over SD-WAN rules
